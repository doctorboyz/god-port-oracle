## Blockers & Resolutions

- **WR ceiling at 53%**: Indicator set has inherent ceiling. Resolved by human accepting 53% based on core philosophy (PF + MaxDD matter more than WR).
- **Volatile regime penalty hurting PF**: Resolved by removing confidence reduction, keeping only label.
- **Hard MTF = Soft MTF**: Discovered halving confidence already pushed counter-trend below threshold. Documented and moved on.

## Honest Feedback

1. **I chased metrics over meaning** — WR=55% was a proxy, not the goal. PF=1.64 with MaxDD=11.8% is a profitable strategy. I should have recognized this at S7 when we hit 53% and made the case to move forward, instead of grinding 24 more configs for 2pp.

2. **Sequential exploration was wasteful** — I could have run ATR multiplier experiments in parallel with session filtering. The ATR discovery was accidental (tried it because nothing else worked), not systematic.

3. **Forward test engine should have been built earlier** — it was always going to be needed for Phase 2. Building it at the end meant I couldn't validate findings during the sweep.

## Lessons Learned

1. **ATR multiplier > indicator tweaks** — Position sizing changes had 5x the impact of indicator weight adjustments. Start with SL/TP optimization before touching signal logic.

2. **Proxy metrics can mislead** — WR=53% with PF=1.64 is better than WR=55% with PF=1.3. The real goal is "port doesn't blow up + grows consistently."

3. **Session filtering is asymmetric** — Reducing Asian confidence helped (+2pp WR). Boosting Overlap didn't matter (already good trades). Filter the bad, don't boost the good.

4. **Forward test validates backtest** — The 4-month forward test (PF=2.79) confirmed the backtest results (PF=1.64). The improvement in forward test suggests the strategy is robust.

5. **When stuck, pivot** — After 24 configs failed to reach WR=55%, the right call was to accept 53% and move to forward testing, not keep grinding.
