## AI Diary (180+ words, first-person)

This session was a turning point for the project. We've been trading for 9 days with learning mode on, collecting data without much thought about quality. When I looked at the numbers, the truth was stark: 488 trades at confidence 0.3-0.5 had a 33% win rate and lost money overall. Those trades weren't just unprofitable — they were actively polluting our data.

The feature importance analysis confirmed what I suspected: ichimoku cloud is the most important indicator family, and SELL is fundamentally different from BUY. The BUY model works (55.6% test accuracy for trending_BUY), but SELL is essentially random (28-34%). This isn't a model problem — it's a data problem. We don't have enough quality SELL signals, and the ones we do have are getting stopped out because the market regime for SELL is harder to predict.

I noticed I was rationalizing the overall model accuracy (86.4% CV) when the test accuracy was only 39%. That's textbook overfitting from correlated time-series data. The 830 trades on May 6 alone share nearly identical market conditions. I should have flagged this immediately instead of being impressed by the CV score.

The config change is the most impactful thing we did: raising confidence thresholds to 0.50/0.55/0.65 and capping positions at 3 per account will dramatically reduce noise trades. The next 14 days of data will be worth more than the previous 9 days combined because every trade will be a qualified signal.

## Honest Feedback (3 friction points)

1. **SSH heredoc quoting continues to waste time** — Running Python analysis through SSH requires careful string escaping. I spent 4-5 iterations fixing quoting issues. Should have written the script locally, SCP'd it, and run it remotely from the start. This is the third session this bites me.

2. **Container env vars don't update on restart** — `docker compose restart` doesn't re-read `.env`. Had to do `down` + `up -d` to get the new config values. Lost 15 minutes debugging why LEARNING_MODE was still 1 after restart. This should be documented in the deployment runbook.

3. **Overfitting wasn't caught fast enough** — The 86.4% CV accuracy on the overall model should have immediately triggered a "this is too good, check test accuracy" response. Instead I presented both numbers without flagging the massive gap. The time-series data has high correlation between adjacent trades, making random CV splits overly optimistic.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Confidence < 0.5 = noise, not signal** — These trades lose money overall (WR 33%, -$93 total). The threshold must be at least 0.50 for any trade to be worth entering or including in ML training data.

2. **BUY and SELL need separate models** — They use completely different features (BUY: ema_200 + obv; SELL: vwap_offset_pct + ichimoku_kijun). Training them together loses the SELL signal entirely.

3. **CV accuracy on time-series is misleading** — 86% CV but 39% test means the model memorized correlated trades. Need time-based split (already implemented in the trainer) and more diverse market conditions.

4. **3 positions max per account reduces noise** — 10 positions at the same time means 10 nearly-identical data points. 3 forces diversification across different market conditions.

## Next Steps

1. Let system collect qualified data for 10-14 days with new config (conf >= 0.50, positions <= 3)
2. After data collection, retrain models — expect trending_BUY to improve significantly with more diverse data
3. SELL strategy needs fundamental rethinking — consider only taking SELL in trending regime with conf >= 0.65
4. Consider adding regime-aware confidence: require higher confidence for SELL in trending, lower for ranging (where SELL WR = 55%)
